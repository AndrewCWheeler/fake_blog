False Pride

Q: What is false pride?
A: An inflated and precarious ego of ungrounded claims to superiority and self-sufficiency that block out the truth and set the foundation and trajectory of one’s relation to the world like unto a man floating in the sky on a slippery baloon. Carried about by the wind, he tries to balance himself, as he shouts at all the inferior human ants below, “I am better than all of you, and I don’t need any of you.” Such self-imposed blindness leads to the inevitable outcome of a sudden and painful pop.

Q: What is the opposite of false pride?
A: An honest and humble ego, that admits its own defeat, asks for help and accepts it, and adopts humble dependence upon a power greater than itself, so that even when injured, it cannot be insulted, because it had nothing insecure left to hide or protect.
